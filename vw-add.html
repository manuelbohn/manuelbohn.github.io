A Global Developmental Psychology

Project Description

The project investigates the longitudinal development of socio-communicative abilities in children between three and five years of age in three cultural settings: Germany, Turkey, Kenya (PI: Prof. Dr. Manuel Bohn). The project is hosted by Leuphana University Lüneburg in Germany and is generously funded by the Volkswagen Foundation. The project follows a large sample of children from each setting (~100 per setting) for two years. At age 3, 4, and 5, children complete a battery of tasks assessing social-cognitive, pragmatic, and language abilities. In addition, we record children’s everyday experiences using wearable cameras. The goal is to better understand the similarities and differences in socio-communicative development between different cultural settings and how they relate to children’s everyday experiences.
